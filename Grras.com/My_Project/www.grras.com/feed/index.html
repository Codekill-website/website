This post is to inform you about women contribution in an open source.

The awards will go to women who make uncommon contributions to open source projects and communities & make creative use of open source technology.

Selection Procedure :

Two candidates will be selected, from the following tracks:

  • Academic: student enrolled in college
  • Community: A woman who volunteers on projects related to open source

 

Award :

Winner will get:

  • Paid registration, flight & hotel accomodations for Red Hat Summit 2016
  • $2,500 stipend
  • Slot as a featured speaker at a future Red Hat Women’s Leadership Community event
  • Feature article on opensource.com

Expectations from Nominees.

  • Programming & Coding
  • Community management and community advocacy
  • User experience (UX), design, marketing & artwork
  • Communications include documentation, tutorials
  • Legal reform & intellectual property advocacy
  • participation in open hardware
  • quality-related contributions like quality assurance, bugs triage
  • Open content
  • Open source methodology
  • System administration
  • Infrastructure

Nominations will be accepted upto 30th Nov, 2015, result will be declared on the base of nomination criteria. After all nominations in the public determine the winners by voting. Winners will be announced at Red Hat Summit 2016, which is going to be held on June 28 – July 1, 2016, in San Francisco.

    ]]>     Benifits of systemctl command over service commands:
    It combines the functionality of both service and chkconfig into a single tool that you can use for instance to enable/disable services permanently or only for the current session.

    How we can use systemctl command(start/stop , enable/disable services)
    1.For start a service immediately:
    systemctl start httpd
    2.For stop a service immediately:
    systemctl stop httpd
    3.For restart a service:
    systemctl restart httpd
    4.Shows status of a service including whether it is running or not:
    systemctl status httpd
    5.Enables a service to be started on bootup(Same as chkconfig command)
    systemctl enable httpd
    6.Disables a service to not start during bootup:
    systemctl disable httpd
    7.Prevent a service from starting dynamically or even manually unless unmasked:
    systemctl mask httpd
    8.Check whether a service is already enabled or not:
    systemctl is-enabled httpd

    Now question arise in mind that service command or chkconfig command is working or not with systemd.
    Yes. It has been modified to call systemctl automatically when dealing with systemd service files. So either of the following commands does the same thing

    systemctl stop httpd

    (or)

    service httpd stop

    Yes, for turning on/off services, compatibility has been provided both ways. chkconfig has been modified to call systemctl when dealing with systemd service files. Also systemctl automatically calls chkconfig when dealing with a traditional sysv init file.
    So either of the following commands does the same thing

    systemctl disable httpd
    (or)
    chkconfig httpd off
